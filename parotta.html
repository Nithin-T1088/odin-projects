How to prepare PAROTTA


There are few culinary creations that capture the heart and palate quite like the Parotta, a layered flatbread that is an absolute masterpiece of texture and taste. Originating in the Indian subcontinent, this is not just bread; it's an ephemeral cloud of dough, crisp on the outside and wonderfully soft, flaky, and tender within. Making a perfect parotta is a meditation in motion, where simple ingredients of flour, water, and oil are transformed through skillful kneading and the rhythmic slapping and folding technique into hundreds of delicate, paper-thin layers. To learn this recipe is to unlock the secret to an astounding and beautiful bread that elevates any meal it accompanies, especially when paired with a rich, aromatic curry that seeps into its buttery folds.


Ingredients :

  • Flour
  • Water or Milk
  • Salt
  • Sugar
  • Oil or Ghee

Procedure :

  1. In a large bowl, mix the flour (Maida/All-Purpose Flour), salt, and sugar.
  2. Slowly add warm water or milk (or the water/milk/egg mixture) and begin to knead. The dough should be soft, smooth, and very pliableâ€”softer than chapati dough.
  3. Coat the dough generously with oil. Cover the bowl with a damp cloth or a lid and let it rest for a minimum of 2 hours (3-4 hours or more is even better).
  4. After resting, gently punch the dough down. Divide it into equal, medium-sized balls (known as beeda).
  5. Place the dough balls in a wide container and coat them completely with a generous amount of oil.
  6. Take one oiled ball and place it on a clean, oiled kitchen counter or work surface. Do not use dry flour.
  7. Once thinly stretched, lift one end of the sheet and begin folding it like an accordion or a paper fan, creating long, thin pleats.
  8. Hold the pleated strip vertically. Start rolling it tightly into a spiral shape (like a coiled rope) and tuck the end underneath the coil.
  9. Place the coiled spirals back into the oiled container and let them rest for a final 15-20 minutes.
  10. Take one spiral coil and gently flatten it with your palm and fingers into a 4-6 inch circle. Avoid using a rolling pin with too much pressure to keep the layers intact.
  11. Heat a tawa (griddle) over medium-high heat. Place the flattened parotta on the tawa. Cook for about a minute until tiny bubbles appear.
  12. Flip the parotta, apply a teaspoon of oil or ghee around the edges and on top. Flip again and press gently with a spatula until both sides are golden brown and crisp.
  13. mmediately after taking the hot parotta off the tawa, place it on a board or counter. Gently "crush" or "slap" the hot parotta together between your palms.
  14. Repeat the process for the remaining dough! Serve hot with your favorite curry (like Salna, Kurma, or Chicken/Mutton Curry).
